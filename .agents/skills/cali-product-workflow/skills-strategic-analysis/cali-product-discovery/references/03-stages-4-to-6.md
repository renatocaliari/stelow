# Stages 4 to 6: Offer, Commitment, and Manual Process

---

## Stage 4 📢 — Develop and Evaluate the Offer

**Objective**: leave the idea field and create a tangible offer. Test the **value risk**: do people demonstrate genuine interest when presented with the proposed solution?

**Tips**:
1. Stages 4 and 5 will potentially be carried out together.
2. Use AI to generate ad ideas for online placement.

### What to Do:
- Develop launch promotions, build trust, and develop bonuses.
- Experiment with different promoters, starting with yourself.
- Experiment with a combination of channels and marketing messages.
- Structure the search for pioneers (early adopters) with a **Pioneer Program**.

### Pioneer Program (Co-Creation Instead of Transaction)
Formalize the search for early adopters who want to help build the solution:
- **Central offer**: extended and free access to the solution (e.g., full annual plan).
- **Clear counterparty**: explicit commitment from the pioneer to provide feedback (e.g., participate in 1 feedback call every X months).

💡 The value offered (complete and free access) attracts people who feel the pain intensely. The assessment of real commitment in this exchange is validated in stage 5.

---

### Types of Promoters

**1. Yourself.**

**2. Customers** — customers who make referrals of your business.

**3. Agencies** — agencies that carry out the prospecting service.

**4. Affiliates** — partners who talk to their own audience about your business:
- Anyone qualified to promote your solution: receives a percentage of the sale.
- **Social media influencers** (Instagram, TikTok) — if you're new to the market, contact small influencers (3–10k followers). Analyze real statistics, types of content, and comments.
  - Offer your solution for free + discount coupon for the audience; or
  - Percentage of sales they make; or
  - Fixed monthly payment to promote X times per week/month.
- Online affiliate platforms: Hotmart, Kiwify, etc.

**5. Employees** — people with the role of carrying out prospecting.

---

### Ways to Advertise

**Warm Approach** (contact with people you know):
- Individual direct message
- Direct message to small group
- Small personalized video showing a quick analysis of something relevant to the person

**Cold Approach** (contact with people you don't know):
- Individual direct message
- Direct message to small group
- Small personalized video showing a quick analysis of something relevant to the person
- 🕵️ Monitor negative comments from competitors: where there's a complaint, there's a potential customer. Enter conversations (social media, Reddit, forums) where users express frustration with an existing solution and present yours as an alternative. ⚠️ Use only with large corporations or well-funded startups.

**Free Content**:
- Own publication: site, blog, podcast, WhatsApp Stories, YouTube channel, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, Substack, Medium.
- **Micro-solutions and free tools**: create a free mini-solution in a similar context and create a link to the paid solution. Offer immense and self-contained value that attracts the target audience. Examples: Chrome extension, AI content generator, niche resource directory, online calculator.
- Content for sites or channels of a niche brand.
- 🎙 Participate as a guest on podcasts/videocasts: smaller ones usually have more slots for guests. During the conversation, when pertinent, demonstrate your solution live.
- **Existing community**: publish content in existing communities without violating rules, delivering contextual value. It's not to talk about your solution, but to deliver value. (Reddit, Facebook groups, Discord and Slack groups, etc.)

**Paid Advertising**:
- Third-party audience: newsletters, podcasts, and videocasts from content creators.
- Online directories: sites that list brands and links in a specific niche.
- References from other brands and solutions: find sites, networks, and solutions in a similar context and negotiate a reference.
- Digital platforms: Google Ads (Google and YouTube), Meta Ads (Facebook and Instagram), TikTok Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Twitter Ads, Pinterest Ads, Reddit Ads, Mercado Livre Ads, OLX Ads.
- Offline platforms: print (newspapers/magazines, leaflets, posters, direct mail), outdoor media (billboards, busdoors, urban furniture, airports, metro, digital panels), radio and TV, events and experiences.
- 🏆 Sponsor hackathons and small niche events: an accessible sponsorship (~$500) guarantees visibility. Ideally, negotiate for your tool to be used by participants during the competition.

**Examples of Media**:
- Phone calls
- Direct messages on social media
- Sending letters or postcards
- SMS text messages
- WhatsApp or Telegram messages
- Audio via WhatsApp or Telegram
- Posts in forums and communities
- Posts on social media
- Door-to-door canvassing
- Direct mail campaigns

### What Risk Are We Mitigating?
Value risk (whether people are interested).

### What to Observe (Signals):
Interest: e.g., curiosity to know more, clicks on ads, email responses, etc.

**Experiment with solutions that have high interest.** Example: at least 60% of the last people contacted demonstrated interest? Before giving up, confirm you're reaching the appropriate audience.

## Stage 5 🤝 — Assess Commitment

**Objective**: go beyond curiosity and test real commitment. Ask people to invest something valuable: **their time or their money**. Separate curious people from true early adopters.

**Tips**:
1. Stages 4 and 5 will potentially be carried out together.
2. Consult the pricing topic in the appendix.

### Methods

#### Pre-Sale
- **Direct pre-sale to small group**: for 10 people you already have a connection with (family, friends, colleagues, referrals) — via in-person conversations, phone, or messengers.
- **Pre-sale to email list**: if you already have an email list related to the topic, create an email with a pre-sale opportunity.
- **Pre-sale on landing page**: create a landing page with pre-sale functionality and observe the number of sales. Tools: Notion (public page), Tally, Gumroad, Instapage, or create with AI.
- **Pre-sale at live event or recorded webinar**: if you chose to do an event/webinar, you can do a pre-sale in it.
- **Lifetime Deals**: to generate initial cash flow and user base quickly. Lifetime access for a single payment with a massive discount. Platforms: AppSumo. ⚠️ Platforms keep a high commission and the customer profile can be demanding. Evaluate with caution.

#### Waitlist
- **👉 Referral**: ask for email for waitlist and then ask for referral of up to 3 people. ⚠️ Very low commitment — serves only as a "sniff" before validating with higher commitment. But by combining with referral, you reach people you wouldn't have access to before. Tool: GetWaitlist. When contacting referred people, inform who referred them to serve as a trust bridge and increase social proof.

- **🪙🏷 Symbolic Investment with Proportional Discount**: ask for email + symbolic investment to enter the list. Offer a discount proportional to the invested amount when the solution is launched. Example: to enter the list, minimum investment of R$50. When launching, discount multiplied by a predetermined factor (e.g., paid R$10, factor 10, gets R$100 discount).

- **🪙💲 Symbolic Investment + Predicted Price + Proportional Discount**: variation above displaying what the predicted value of the solution is. Validates closer to reality, as people know the price beforehand. Allows changing the predicted value and seeing if more people invest the minimum amount — signal of the balance between business financial needs and the audience.

- **🏷🎯 Discount with Correct Guess — Price Discovery**: ask for email for the list and ask the person to guess the price of the solution. If they guess within a 10% interval up or down, they get 30% off at launch. Allows discovering the price people are willing to pay.

#### Other Methods
- **Marketplace ad**: advertise and observe sales (Facebook Marketplace, Mercado Livre, OLX, Reddit, etc.)
- **Contact after interview**: ask for email and phone right after interviewing about the solution to offer when it's ready. The last part (offer when ready) is important to know the level of commitment.
- **App**: a mobile app with just one basic functionality to measure number of downloads and opens.
- **Fake Door**: measure intention without having the product or feature. People believe that by "knocking on the door" they can "enter" — but there's nothing behind it yet. ⚠️ When someone "knocks", deliver some value to them. Delight, even without having a solution.
- **Influencer**: offer free to an influential person in the niche, either a prototype or service for an experimentation period. In exchange, you can negotiate that they share with the audience about the future solution (with % of future sales, or don't ask for anything and enhance natural sharing).

#### Pioneer Program in Exchange for Feedback
Validates the commitment to use and collaborate. While pre-sale tests willingness to pay, here you test willingness to invest time and attention.

**How it works**: execute the Pioneer Program offer defined in stage 4. For a limited group of users, grant free access and in exchange they commit to being an active source of insights.

**What to observe**:
- **Adhesion rate**: how many of the contacted pioneers accept the terms (access in exchange for feedback)?
- **Quality of commitment**: do they actually show up for calls? Respond to surveys? Is the feedback superficial or in-depth?

A pioneer who pays with time and ideas can be as committed as one who pays with money.

## Stage 6 📝 — Discover the Process

**Objective**: test the **execution and usability risk** — can you actually deliver the solution? Instead of jumping to complete development, experiment with a "valuable manual process".

**Tip**: Only skip this stage if you (1) already have sufficient personal knowledge of the process, or (2) have a strong assumption allied with resources to automate or produce a new simplified solution with the same time and money it would take to discover the process. **In general, carry out this stage.**

> Don't underestimate the real world: you'll probably spend more time and money trying to develop something if you skip this stage, if you don't have agreed limits.

### What to Do:
Systematize manual or outsourced solutions that allow learning the complete process that people will use to solve the problem with the new solution and how it will fit into their context. **Systematize the process before "productizing".**

> Sahil Lavingia: *"As you complete the first cycle with clients, document every part of the process so that with each subsequent client, you have a guide. This document will be the true MVP of your business — not the minimum viable product we're all trying to build and launch. I'm talking about the valuable manual process that precedes it and will be the foundation for the business you're trying to build. Methodically."*

Remember to use the 6 principles for experimentation.

### Examples of Valuable Manual Process

**Personal Assistant (Concierge)**: do it together with the client, and if possible, live. Identify and offer the best solution for them and receive feedback.

**Artisanal Backstage (Flintstoning or Wizard of Oz)**: offer a solution that looks automated to the person, but you'll do it manually backstage. Identify bottlenecks and risks.

**Repackaging**: use of another company's product or service with your own "packaging". Identify if there's already something on the market that solves well and what points for improvement exist.

**Solution Integration**: offer an integration of several existing solutions, especially digital ones. Tools: Windmill.dev, N8N, ActivePieces, Zapier, Make.com.

### What Risk Are We Mitigating?
- "Execution" or "realization" risk (feasibility — whether you're capable of solving the problem)
- Usability risk (whether people will know how to use it)

### What to Observe (Signals)?
Evaluate if it's possible to repeat the process with more people maintaining quality and depending less on your specific performance. That is, could other people solve it following the same process you followed? And even better: could you automate it by integrating solutions?

## ⚠️ High-Risk and Consequence Systems: Integration and Systemic Validation

This section applies to a category of solutions that requires an extra layer of rigor before evolving to a viable business.

### Complicated vs. Complex Systems

**Complicated system**: like an airplane engine. Components and rules with linear and predictable interactions. The risk lies in the quality of execution.
Examples: software for medical equipment, financial transaction systems, platforms with sensitive fiscal/legal data, autonomous vehicle control. A failure is not a surprise — it's a calculation or implementation error.

**Complex system**: like an ecosystem. Interconnected components with emergent and often unpredictable behavior. The risk emerges from unanticipated interactions.
Examples: social network, recommendation algorithm that creates filter bubbles, products with AI models that learn and adapt continuously.

For these cases, jumping from stage 6 to stage 7 is not just risky; **it can be irresponsible**. The biggest risk is not just financial, but also operational, legal, and human.

The focus shifts from just "building something that nobody wants" to also **"avoiding building something that causes harm in ways we can't control"**.

### What to Do:

**1. Modular Development and Contained Experimentation**
Each module goes through its own validation cycle (stages 1 to 6 on a smaller scale) before being integrated.

**2. Integration and System Testing**
Connect the validated modules and test the connections between them.

- For **complicated systems**: focus on verification of compliance, absence of failures, and adherence to regulations.
- For **complex systems**: focus on detection of emergent behaviors, vulnerabilities, and unintended consequences. Validation focuses on **robustness of containment and control mechanisms**. Questions to answer:
  - Are our early warning systems effective?
  - Do our automatic "emergency brakes" to contain an escalation of damage work?
  - Are the controls we give users to manage their experience and protect themselves clear and accessible?

**Process**: start by integrating two modules, test them exhaustively, then add a third, and so on. Document how the addition of each new component affects the system as a whole.

**3. Simulation in a Controlled Environment (Staging/Sandbox)**
Before any contact with the public or real production environment, the integrated system must run in a simulation environment that mirrors real conditions.

**Purpose**: observe behavior over time, submit to stress, allow an internal team (or very restricted group of expert pioneers) to interact in a safe environment.

**Signals to observe**: stability, resilience (how the system recovers from failures) and, above all, the **absence of damage or negative consequences**.

Only after this rigorous process is it possible to advance with more safety to stage 7.
