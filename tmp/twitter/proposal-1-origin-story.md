1/ stelow. an invitation to builders.
i've been building products as dev, pm, consultant and builder. one thing always bugged me: the gap between "i have an idea" and "let's build it" is way too fuzzy.


2/ some teams jump straight to solutions. before aligning on the problem, constraints, or what success actually looks like. others overplan an imaginary future with detailed specs that create more unfinished work than shipped outcomes.


3/ and now with ai, it's common to jump straight into generating polished html/css prototypes, skipping ux exploration entirely. no comparison. no tradeoff analysis. just burning tokens on the first idea.


4/so i built stelow. an opinionated product workflow for ai agents and humans. 

it's not a project management tool. it's not a document generator. it's an orchestrator. it guides the workflow, keeps state, uses tools agnostically. 


5/ the workflow and approaches come from strategies real product teams use. works for greenfield and brownfield projects alike. 


6/ the workflow goes through phases: appetite & mode → shape (in/out) → adversarial critique → @plannotator gate → interface exploration → tech planning → execution → verification.


7/you describe what you want to build. the workflow takes it from there. depending on the selected mode, the workflow pauses at key moments and waits for user feedback or approval.


8/ during interface exploration, the workflow evaluates 1-5 ascii archetypes with tradeoffs, then creates a hybrid combining the best parts. no wasted design work, no skipping.


9/ i'm dogfooding this on my side projects. running the same workflow on my own work.  this is still early. 


10/ i'm inviting builders (solo or teams, experienced or not) to try it and tell me what sucks. no sales pitch. no landing page. just a workflow and a /sw-start. feedback shapes what comes next.
